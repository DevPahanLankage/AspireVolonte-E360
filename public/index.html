Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common insurance and investment questions

What types of insurance do I need?

The insurance you need depends on your life stage and circumstances. Generally, we recommend having health insurance as a foundation, life insurance if you have dependents, and critical illness/disability insurance to protect your income.

How much life insurance coverage should I get?

A general rule is 10-15 times your annual income, but this varies based on your debts, dependents, and future financial obligations. Our advisors can help you calculate the right amount for your situation.

What's the difference between term and whole life insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (e.g., 20 years) with lower premiums. Whole life insurance provides lifetime coverage and builds cash value but has higher premiums.

Can I have multiple insurance policies?

Yes, you can have multiple insurance policies. In fact, it's often recommended to have different types of coverage to create a comprehensive protection plan.

Understanding Investment Accounts

Make informed decisions about your financial future

Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)

A TFSA allows you to earn investment income tax-free throughout your lifetime. Ideal for both short and long-term savings goals.

  • Tax-free investment growth
  • Flexible withdrawal options
  • No tax on withdrawals
  • Contribution room reinstated after withdrawal
2024 Contribution Limit: $7,000

Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)

An RRSP is designed to help you save for retirement while providing immediate tax benefits through contribution deductions.

  • Tax-deductible contributions
  • Tax-deferred growth
  • Higher contribution limits
  • Ideal for retirement planning
2024 Contribution Limit: 18% of previous year's income
